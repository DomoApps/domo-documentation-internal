### Intro

Custom apps are often beyond the scope of support. However, there are a few steps you can take to troubleshoot the app and help the customer.

It's helpful to keep these two things in mind:

- We should err on the side of helping the customer and getting them to their next destination, even if it's outside of the usual support scope.
- Customers should not be passed around with no clear direction on how to solve their problem.

### Collect Information

Before any escalation or handoff, collect the following information about the problem and be ready to communicate it:

- Is the Domo platform working?
  - Can this be considered PAT related based on Domo's definition of a PAT?
- Is the DataSet available and accessible?
  - Does PDP exist on the DataSet? Is the person submitting the ticket included in an appropriate PDP policy?
  - Is this federated data? The customer may be responsible to restore access in this case.
  - Can a regular Table Card be built on the DataSet? Does the data come through from the DataSet?
- Asset Library Owner
  - Has this app been correctly shared with the ticket submitter?
- What is the new version of the app? A new version may be available.
- Domo Bricks
  - What DDX/Domo Brick did the customer use? Get a link to the brick.
    - Perform diff checks on the brick versus the customer's code to see if it differs.
- Is the app really a custom Dev Studio app (not an app purchased through the Domo Appstore)? If the functionality works outside of Domo and doesn't work in Domo, continue to troubleshoot as Domo may be the factor causing an issue. If the app does not meet this criterion, you may need to direct the customer to contact the app owner to further troubleshoot.

<Note>**Note:** If the customer is building a custom app and has questions about the process, let them know that this is outside the scope of support and provide Knowledge Base documentation. If they would like help from Domo, Consulting would need to step in. If they want to move forward with Consulting, contact their Customer Success Manager and provide them with all of the relevant information.

**Note:** A CSM may choose to have a TSM take a look, but it is up to them to decide. Support should not suggest solutions to the customer but instead connect them to the CSM. After completing the warm handoff to the CSM and Consulting is working with the customer, you can close the case.</Note>
